I'm a Sabarmati Bridge Fellow at IIT Gandhinar, currently working on the collective dissipation of dipole oscillators.

My current work involves understanding light-matter interaction in various strong interaction regimes and observing Non-Markovian effects in the weak coupling regime. I am also interested in the reservoir dynamics, which are traced out in Markovian treatments, as Non-Markovian effects such as retardation and Poincare recurrences play out inside the reservoir. For a system of two dipole oscillators in an ideal cavity it is possible to exactly compute the bath dynamics in terms of observables such electric field and Intensity inside the cavity. The adjacent plots show the Intensity distribution inside the cavity for the dipole oscillators initially in a (a) subradiant and (b) super-radiant state placed at emitter wavelength separation. We can see that the trapping and fast decay occur after an initial time Non-Markovian propagation delay for the subradiant and superradiant case respectively
